Music and Moods

Do you ever listen to specific song or band when you are sad?

Do you ever listen to a certain genre music when you are trying to focus and study for an exam?

Have you ever watched a movie without background music? It was probably very boring.

Why? Because music evokes moods and emotions.

Try and think of the last time music was not a part of your daily routine. You probably can’t. Why is this?

Most people unconsciously like the stimulation music triggers in our minds. Research shows evidence that certain musical patterns stimulates certain moods and emotions within the brain . In particular, complex musical patterns stimulate the same areas of the brain that are responsible for the pleasure we get from from eating, exercising, drug usage, and the like . Since musical patterns are complex though, certain music genres and songs are able to elicit many different emotions (not just pleasure)

How can music cause emotional reactions?

Music is composed of several features out of which some of the major components it consists of are:

  • Acousticness
  • Danceability
  • Energy/Arousal
  • Mode
  • Loudness
  • Valence
  • Depth

So which component has impact on your mood?

The three predominant qualities of a song or musical pattern that are able to do this are: valence, arousal, and depth

  • Valence (Spectrum of emotions in music): A measure from 0.0 to 1.0 describing the musical positiveness conveyed by a track. Tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g. happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry).

  • Energy/Arousal (Intensity and energy in music):Energy is a measure from 0.0 to 1.0 and represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy. For example, death metal has high energy, while a Bach prelude scores low on the scale. Perceptual features contributing to this attribute include dynamic range, perceived loudness, timbre, onset rate, and general entropy.

  • Depth is defined as the “intellect and sophistication in music”
